Frequently Asked Questions

CRE investing demystified — straight answers for serious investors.

What is a good cap rate in today's market?+
In 2025, cap rates have expanded 100–200bps from 2021–22 lows. A 6%+ cap rate is generally solid for residential rentals. For commercial: Multifamily 5–7%, Retail 6–8%, Industrial 5–7%, Office 7–10% (due to vacancy risk). Always compare to your local submarket — cap rates vary significantly by metro and asset class.
What DSCR do lenders require?+
Most commercial lenders require a minimum DSCR of 1.25x — meaning the property generates 25% more NOI than required to cover annual debt service. Some lenders will go to 1.20x on strong assets. DSCR below 1.0x means the deal loses money before any equity returns, and no bank will lend. Use our DSCR calculator to test your numbers at different loan terms.
What's the difference between cap rate and cash-on-cash return?+
Cap rate is financing-agnostic — it measures the property's income potential relative to value (NOI ÷ Price). Cash-on-cash return measures your actual cash yield on money invested (pre-tax cash flow ÷ cash invested). A deal can have a 7% cap rate but only a 4% cash-on-cash if you're using a high LTV loan at a high interest rate. You need both numbers to understand a deal properly.
How do you calculate IRR on a CRE investment?+
IRR (Internal Rate of Return) is the annualized return accounting for all cash flows AND the exit sale, including the time value of money. Typical targets by strategy: Core (6–9%), Core-Plus (9–12%), Value-Add (12–18%), Opportunistic (18%+). Use the IRR tab in our calculator — enter purchase price, down payment, NOI, growth rate, hold period, and exit cap rate to get your projected IRR and equity multiple.
What does your deal analysis service include?+
Our full underwriting covers: cap rate, DSCR, cash-on-cash, IRR, equity multiple, sensitivity analysis, comparable sales, submarket analysis, and a go/no-go recommendation with written rationale. We also flag any red flags in the rent roll, expense ratios, or financing structure. Reports are lender-ready and typically delivered within 48 hours of receiving deal details.
Do you work with first-time CRE investors?+
Yes. Many of our clients are transitioning from residential investing or buying their first commercial property. We bring institutional-quality analysis to every deal regardless of size. Our free calculators are also available to anyone — no sign-up required. For hands-on guidance, submit a deal inquiry and we'll scope the right level of support for your situation.
What is a 1031 exchange and should I use one?+
A 1031 exchange lets you defer capital gains tax when selling an investment property by reinvesting into a like-kind property. Key rules: 45 days to identify a replacement, 180 days to close, equal or greater value required, must use a qualified intermediary. It's almost always worth doing if you're selling and planning to reinvest — the tax deferral compounds significantly over time. We can help you evaluate timing and structure.
